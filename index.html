Hi, I'm Hannah.

About Me

I’m a second-year combined major in Biochemistry and Data Science at Northeastern University. I became drawn to this field because I’m interested in genetics, drug development, and cancer research. I am also fascinated by the intersection of biology and technology; how biological discovery and medical research can be strengthened through data-driven approaches, especially in bioinformatics and biotechnology. During my first semester, I had the opportunity to study abroad in Thessaloniki, Greece, which helped me become more independent and adaptable while balancing a rigorous academic schedule in a new environment. On campus, I’m involved in Kappa Kappa Gamma, Oasis NEU, and the Biochemistry Club, which has helped me build strong collaboration and communication skills outside the classroom.

My academic path has taken me from molecular biology labs to data science coursework, and I've come to believe that some of the most interesting problems live exactly at that intersection. Whether it's analyzing genetic sequences to authenticate the food supply, modeling how climate change expands the range of disease-carrying mosquitoes, or studying tumors in endangered sea turtles, the questions that motivate me are ones where biological complexity meets computational tools.

On the data side, I work in Python and R, with experience in machine learning, statistical modeling, and data visualization. I've built interactive visualizations using Altair and D3.js, implemented machine learning algorithms from scratch, and worked with datasets ranging from genomic sequences to ecological surveys. I'm particularly interested in how visualization can communicate scientific findings to audiences who aren't specialists.

On the biology side, I have hands-on laboratory experience with DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis, and barcoding techniques. I've applied these to real-world questions: verifying that fish sold in markets actually match their labeled species, a problem with significant public health and environmental implications.
