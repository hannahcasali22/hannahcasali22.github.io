Projects & Research

My work spans molecular biology, data science, and software development. What ties these projects together is a shared interest in using methods, such as computational or experimental, to answer questions that have real consequences. Below are some highlights.

Genomics · Lab Work and Computational Methods

DNA Barcoding & Fish Authentication

Investigated seafood mislabeling by extracting DNA from market fish samples, amplifying the COI gene via PCR, and comparing sequences against the BOLD database. The project combined wet lab techniques with bioinformatics to assess whether labeled species matched their actual genetic identity — a question relevant to both consumer protection and ecosystem monitoring.

Key techniques used:

  • DNA extraction and PCR amplification
  • Gel electrophoresis for quality verification
  • Sequence alignment and BLAST/BOLD database querying
  • Statistical analysis of match scores and species identifications
Ecology · Epidemiology

Climate Change & Vector-Borne Disease

Researched how rising temperatures and shifting precipitation patterns are altering the geographic range and seasonal activity of disease vectors such as mosquitoes and ticks. Synthesized literature on species distribution modeling and used climate projection datasets to assess how endemic zones for diseases like dengue fever and Lyme disease may expand under different warming scenarios.

This project sharpened my skills in reading epidemiological literature, working with large geospatial datasets, and communicating probabilistic projections clearly to a non-specialist audience.

Marine Biology · Conservation

Fibropapillomatosis in Sea Turtles

Contributed to a research investigation into fibropapillomatosis (FP), a tumor disease affecting green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas) globally. FP prevalence has been linked to environmental stressors including water quality degradation and pollutant exposure. The project involved reviewing disease surveillance data and analyzing patterns in tumor severity across different habitats.

Oasis NEU · Web

Mix Match - Virtual Closet Web Application

Designed and developed a full-stack web application that lets users catalog clothing items, filter by attribute, and plan outfits. The project involved front-end interface design, back-end data management, and thinking carefully about user experience for a real-world organizational tool.
